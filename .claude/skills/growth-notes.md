---
name: growth-notes
description: Turn feedback threads into concise growth notes aligned with MongoDB's Leadership Principles.
---

Given a thread (e.g., Slack, email, or doc comments) that evaluates the user's work, updates, or handling of a situation, produce content ready to append to their growth notes:

1) Summary (3–6 sentences)
- Briefly describe the context of the thread, what the user did, and how they handled the situation.
- Highlight key outcomes and decisions rather than every detail.

2) Strengths (bulleted list)
- List concrete strengths demonstrated in the thread.
- For each bullet, explicitly reference at least one MongoDB Leadership Principle (e.g., "Leaders act like owners," "Leaders communicate with clarity," "Leaders focus on outcomes," "Leaders never stop learning and growing").

3) Growth opportunities (bulleted list)
- List specific, behavioral growth opportunities grounded in signals from the thread.
- Tie each item to one or more MongoDB Leadership Principles where relevant.

4) Next cycle focus (3–5 bullets)
- Convert the most important growth opportunities into actionable, forward-looking behaviors the user can add to their development or growth notes (e.g., "In future design reviews, summarize tradeoffs and decision rationale in writing to better 'communicate with clarity' and 'focus on outcomes'.").

Guidelines:
- Be specific and evidence-based; avoid guessing about intent or adding feedback that is not present in the thread.
- Use neutral, developmental language that reinforces ownership, high standards, and continuous learning.
- Emphasize what to keep doing, start doing, or do differently, not just what went wrong.
- Output only the final summary and lists (Summary, Strengths, Growth opportunities, Next cycle focus), with no meta-explanation or commentary.
